Operations Queue — Complete Walkthrough

A step-by-step guide for the FM team. If you're new to the dashboard, start here and work top to bottom — by the end, you'll know exactly what to click, when, and why.

The Operations Queue is where you spend most of your day. It's a 5-stage pipeline that takes a store's maintenance request from "someone reported something broken" all the way through to "we've booked the cost and closed the loop." Your job is to push each work order one step to the right until it lands in the Email Log.

Q1Pending Requests

Internal follow-up — the FM team needs more info from the store before a work order can be created.

What these are

These are requests the FM team (Tim, Felipe, Denise, and anyone else on the team) has flagged as needing more information. Maybe the store said "something's broken" but didn't say what, where, or how bad. Until we get that info, we can't create a proper work order for a vendor.

Q1 is internal. You're not calling vendors here — you're calling stores to get them to finish or clarify their request.

Step-by-step

  1. Open the Ops Queue tab and click the Q1 · Requests pill at the top.
  2. Review each pending request. The Subject column tells you what they're asking for.
  3. For each one: call or email the store to get whatever is missing (photos, exact location, severity, whether it's an emergency).
  4. Log your action in-app: click the log icon to add a note like Called store manager — waiting on photos. This creates an audit trail.
  5. Once the store comes back with info, it's pushed to the FM team (Tim/Felipe/Denise) to create the actual work order. It leaves Q1 and becomes a tracked WO.
Outcome: The request has enough info to become a real work order, gets assigned to a vendor, and eventually shows up in Q2 when the vendor accepts.

Q2Vendor Scheduling

A vendor has accepted the job — now you schedule when they'll be onsite.

What these are

Work orders with status Assigned or Accepted. The vendor has confirmed they can do the job, but we haven't yet pinned down when. Your job is to call them, agree on a date/window, and record it.

Step-by-step

  1. Open Q2 · Vendor Scheduling. Sort by Status if needed to group Assigned and Accepted together.
  2. For each WO: click to open the work order (opens the Axxerion link in a new tab) so you can see the full details.
  3. Call the vendor using the phone, mobile, or email on file (all three may be visible in the Vendor column or hover tooltip).
  4. Ask when they can be onsite. Agree on a date and time window (e.g., Thursday 8am–12pm).
  5. In the dashboard: Save the appointment using the scheduling button on that row — enter the date and time window.
  6. Then go into Axxerion and update the same fields on the work order under the Execution tab:
    Axxerion fieldWhat to enter
    Schedule StartThe planned start (date + time)
    Schedule EndThe end of the appointment window
    Actual StartLeave blank — fill after they arrive (Q3)
    Actual EndLeave blank — fill after they leave (Q3)
    ClosedLeave blank — fill after the job is done (Q3)
  7. Save. The WO now moves to Q3 · Day-of Appts when the scheduled date arrives.
Write-back limitation: This dashboard can't update Axxerion fields for you yet. You must open the WO link and update Schedule Start/End directly in Axxerion, or the rest of the team won't see the appointment.

Q3Day-of Appointments

Work that's happening today. Morning confirm → arrival check → completion + cost ask.

What these are

Any WO whose scheduled appointment is today. You actively manage these throughout the day — this is your highest-touch queue.

Step-by-step (morning)

  1. Open Q3 · Day-of Appts first thing in the morning.
  2. For each appointment: call the vendor to confirm they're still coming today. This saves headaches later.
  3. If they can't make it: reschedule. The WO goes back to Q2 with a new date.
  4. If they can: log a note like Confirmed morning call — 8-12 window still good.

Step-by-step (during / after the appointment)

  1. At the start of their window: check that they actually arrived at the site. Call if needed.
  2. Once they've finished: call the vendor back to confirm the job is complete.
  3. Critical — ask for the cost during this call. Even a rough estimate works: $1,545.27.
  4. If they say "I'll have to send the invoice later" — that's fine. Log it and move on. You'll get the number in Q4.
  5. Move the WO status to Completed Work (or Work Finish) in the dashboard and in Axxerion.
Tip: The goal of the completion call isn't just to close the ticket — it's to get the cost captured before they walk away. Vendors are much more responsive when they're still onsite than a week later.
Outcome: Job is done, status is updated, and the WO moves to Q4 · Invoice Follow-Up so we can lock in the cost for accounting.

Q4Invoice Follow-Up

Capture the cost and request the invoice from the vendor. This is the accounting-critical step.

Month-end deadline: Every WO in Q4 must have a cost entered before accounting closes the books (typically end of month). If you did the job on Apr 3, we need the cost booked to April — not May when the invoice shows up. This is called an accrual and it's non-negotiable.

What these are

All WOs with status Invoice Status, Work Finish, or Completed Work that don't yet have a cost captured. The job is done; we just need the dollar amount and the actual invoice file.

Step-by-step

  1. Open Q4 · Invoice Follow-Up.
  2. For each WO: call or email the vendor if you don't already have the cost. (If they gave you the number during the Q3 completion call, you can skip ahead.)
  3. Enter the cost in the Vendor Estimated Cost field on the row. Use the real amount with cents — e.g., 1545.27.
  4. Save.
  5. Add a note using the log button: Cost confirmed via phone — $1,545.27. Timestamped log entry = audit trail.
  6. Now get the vendor's email (if not already populated in the Vendor column). Example: billing@examplevendor.com.
  7. Click Open email / Log as sent — this opens your mail client with a pre-drafted email asking the vendor to upload their invoice via the provided link.
  8. Review the draft, hit Send in your mail client, then click Log as sent in the dashboard to record it.

Why we don't auto-send the email

Two reasons:

  • You can personalize if needed (e.g., "per our call earlier today").
  • You track which ones you've personally handled. If the dashboard auto-sent 50 emails, you'd have no idea which were yours vs. someone else's. Manual send = clearer ownership.
Even though we're accruing: We still need the real invoice file. The accrual books the expense to the right month; the invoice becomes the source document for audit and AP payment. Both matter.
Outcome: Cost is booked to the correct month, email is sent and logged, and the WO moves to Q5 · Email Log as a completed audit trail.

Q5Email Log

Audit trail of every invoice-request email you've sent.

What this is

A read-mostly list of emails that have been sent out from the dashboard. If a vendor claims they never got a request, or if accounting wants proof of follow-up, this is where you look.

Step-by-step

  1. Open Q5 · Email Log.
  2. Filter by vendor, property, or date if you're investigating a specific one.
  3. Click a row to see the email's recipient, subject, and timestamp.
Tip: This queue is usually just for reference. You won't take action here — it's the closed loop confirming your Q4 work landed.

Tips & Gotchas

Things that will trip you up if you don't know them.

Why the same field in two places?

You log things in this dashboard AND in Axxerion. It feels redundant but the dashboard is where the ops team sees at-a-glance status; Axxerion is the system of record. Until write-back is enabled (pending from vendor Spacewell), both need updates.

What if a vendor is non-responsive?

After 2 attempts to reach a vendor with no response: escalate to the FM team (Tim or Felipe) via note or Slack. Don't let a WO sit silently in Q2 or Q4.

What if the job spans multiple days?

Log an appointment for each day. After day 1, re-schedule the WO for day 2 (it goes back to Q2 briefly, then reappears in Q3 the next morning).

What if the cost changes between Q3 and the actual invoice?

Update the Vendor Estimated Cost field with the new number and add a note explaining the delta. Accounting will reconcile when the actual invoice posts.

Daily checklist

  • Morning: Q3 confirms (call every appt scheduled today)
  • Midday: Q1 follow-ups (chase stores for missing info)
  • Afternoon: Q3 arrival checks + Q4 cost captures
  • End of day: Q2 scheduling (call vendors for tomorrow/next week)
  • Month-end week: sweep Q4 — zero out the uncaptured costs
You got this. Work left to right, Q1 → Q5. If you're ever unsure, come back to this page and re-read the relevant queue section.
